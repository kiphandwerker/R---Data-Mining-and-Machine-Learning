# Split data into Training and Test sample

B.) Classification Tree



The classification tree produced 3 variables as the most significant: field goals made (FGM), rebounds made (REB), and and games played (GamesPLayed). If an NBA player had a FGM > 1.85, REB > 1.75, or played > 55.5 games, they were predicted to have played 5 or more years in the NBA. See tree.err for the misclassification rate for the model.

E.) Gradient Boosted Tree



The gradient boosted tree showed that games played (GamesPlayed) was the most important predictor, followed by field goals made (FGM) as the most important variable in predicting if a player played 5 or more years in the NBA. See bs.err for the misclassification rate for the model.

# Gradient Boosted Tree

y = as.numeric(NBA.merged$y[train])-1
NBA.merged.tr = data.frame(y,NBA.merged[train,-20])
bs.NBA.merged = gbm(y~.,data=NBA.merged.tr,distribution="bernoulli",
n.trees=62,interaction.depth=1,cv.folds = 0)

summary(bs.NBA.merged)
